Devraz Bhairav

Nunar's name originates from Nar from Narayana, meaning the abode of Lord Vishnu. Devraz Bairav is revered as the chief of all eight Barabas of Lord Shiva. Before independence, Nunar was the base camp for the annual Harmukh Ganga Yatra (Ganga Ashtami). It was the largest Hindu-populated village in the historical Srinagar district.

Our timeline

  1. 1940

    Devraz Bairav existed in the form of an idol and a Shiv Ling under a Kashmiri Bargad (Brann) tree in the open. Meanwhile the youths of Nunar formed a committee through a proper voting process, and Sh. Laxmanji Pandita, son of the late Raja Pandit, was elected as president.

  2. 1945

    After completing 5 year term, elections were held again and Late Sh. Prathvi Nath Pandita was elected as president. It is during his tenure that the present standing Dharamshalla was constructed at Vedsewar temple. A rath yatra of Lord Krishna was started from vedsewar mandir to Devraz Bairav temple on janamashtami annually.

  3. 1963

    Under the Presidentship of Late Jawahar Lal Koul, a boundary wall was constructed around Devraz Bhairav

  4. 1963

    Asthapna sudhar samiti Nunar, came in existence under the presidentship of late Balji Sharma and petron late Dina Nath Zutshi

  5. 1982

    The present standing Devraz Bhairav temple was constructed
